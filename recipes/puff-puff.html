West African Puff-Puff

What is Puff-Puff?

Most West African countries are familiar with the popular street food Puff-Puff, which is known by differemt names depending on the country. Ghanaians call it Bofrot (togbei), and the Cameroonians and Nigerias call it Puff-Puff or beignet in French. The basic ingredient consists of flour, sure, yeast, water, salt, and oil for deep frying. it is an easy and quikc recipe to make with different variations. it is doughnut-like but is slightly more chewy. It is typically eaten as a street-side snack

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 cups Flour
  • 2 1/4 teaspoon of yeast
  • 2 cups warm water
  • 1/2 - 3/4 cups of sugar
  • cooking oil
  • 1/2 tablespoon of salt

Instructions

  1. Activate the yeast: Mix salt, water, and yeast. Set it aside for 5 minutes
  2. Make the dough: Add flour and mix
  3. Rest and rise: Let the mixture rise for approx. 1-2 hours.
  4. Pre-heat the oil: In a large saucepan, pour vegetable oil into a pot until it's about 3 inches high and place in low heat
  5. Check the heat: Test to make sure the oil is hot enough by putting a drop of batter into the oil. if the oil is not hot enough, the batter will stay at the bottom instead of rising to the top.
  6. Fry the puff puff: When the oil is hot enough, use a spoon to dish up the batter and another spoon or spatula to drop it in the oil. Sort of in the shape of a ball.
  7. Check the doneness: Fry for minutes until the bottom side is golden brown.
  8. Flip the puff puff: Turn the ball over for a few minutees until the inderside is golden brown
  9. Remove from oil: Use a large sppoon or a slotted spoon to take it out of the oil. Place on napkins to help soak up the excess oil.
  10. Coat the Puff PuffThis step isn't necessary, but you can roll up the finished products in table sugar or powdered sugar to make it sweeter
